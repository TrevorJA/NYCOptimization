
The following are the hypothesized and planned research contributions. The final contributions will be contingent on the results of the study.

1. This study contributes `hazard_filling`, a simulation-free, coverage-designed selection of the MOEA search ensemble that deliberately over-represents severe hydrologic hazard conditions (drought-event metrics, flood metrics, low-flow indices computed on each realized 10-year sequence), and tests it head-to-head against independent and identically distributed probabilistic sampling from the same stationary generator (`fixed_probabilistic`) at matched ensemble size N, record length L, and function-evaluation budget on a real multi-reservoir system. Because the two designs draw from one stationary population and differ only in the rule that selects which realizations enter the ensemble, `fixed_probabilistic` is the exact statistical control for `hazard_filling`, and the contrast is the core of RQ1. The comparison is made only under a held-out, deeply uncertain re-evaluation (a Latin hypercube over the CMIP6 harmonic forcing box crossed with stochastic realizations), so re-evaluation is a test of generalization to conditions absent from search. A replication scheme (K ensemble draws x S search seeds, with the draw as the unit of analysis) separates ensemble-construction variance from search variance.

   Differentiators to keep:
   - vs Cohen et al. (2021): simulation-free hazard coordinates, computable on the sequences alone, versus a problem-driven baseline-regret property that requires one perfect-foresight optimization per candidate scenario.
   - vs Bonham et al. (2024): space-filling selection moved into the search phase rather than applied post hoc to robustness ranking.
   - vs Zatarain Salazar et al. (2017): multi-dimensional, tail-over-representing coverage versus one-dimensional, probability-preserving flow stratification.
   - vs Eker and Kwakkel (2018): the null benchmark for designed selection, upgraded to hazard space, a real system, and a powered draw-based replication, versus outcome-space diversity, the lake problem, and solution counting.

2. This study demonstrates the potential to improve DRB reservoir outcomes through re-optimization of the existing FFMP rule structure (RQ2), and probes whether a variable-resolution FFMP with more storage zones improves performance or robustness (RQ3).

(The input-space redundancy observation, that distinct generator parameterizations can yield realizations with overlapping hazards, is retained only as brief motivation for why hazard-space selection is interesting, not as a tested contrast. The previously scoped `resampled_probabilistic`, `input_stratified`, and `hazard_filling_du` designs, and the "hazard coverage beats input coverage" claim, are out of the campaign and appear, if at all, only as future work.)
