
The following are the hypothesized and planned research contributions. The final contributions will be contingent on the results of the study.

1. This project demonstrates that, during MOEA optimization, evaluation of potential solutions (sets of decision variables) using a uniform subsampling of the multi-dimensional hazard space (drought metrics, flood metric, low-flow metrics, etc) rather than a standard probabilistic ensemble will result in pareto front solutions that are more robust under re-evaluation under a larger set of uncertain conditions. (Framing: Cohen et al. 2021 already established that search-set composition moves out-of-sample robustness; the contribution here is the scalable, simulation-free, coverage-designed construction and its test under a genuinely held-out deep-uncertainty re-evaluation — see the gap statement in `literature/scenario_design.md`.)

2. This study demonstrates the potential to improve DRB reservoir outcomes through re-optimization of the existing FFMP rule structure.
