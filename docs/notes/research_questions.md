Primary research questions for this study:

1. (Core) Does constructing the search ensemble by space-filling coverage of the hydrologic hazard space, rather than by independent and identically distributed sampling from the same stationary generator, change the robustness of the resulting Pareto-approximate policies under held-out, deeply uncertain re-evaluation?

2. Can re-optimizing the parameters of the existing FFMP rule structure improve NYC and basin-wide outcomes (water supply reliability, Montague and Trenton flow-target compliance, downstream flood exposure, and storage resilience) relative to current operations?

3. Does a variable-resolution FFMP structure with more storage zones improve performance or robustness relative to the current zone structure? (The variable-resolution sweep, FFMP-n for n in {8, 10, 12}, is fixed against the compute allocation rather than run under every design.)
