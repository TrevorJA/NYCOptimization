# Deep-uncertainty optimization workflows (water-supply pathways)

*Paper 3 literature note. Annotated summaries of items in the Zotero collection "Paper 3 NYC Reoptimization" (`ISYGLK35`). Last updated 2026-06-16.*

Scope: the end-to-end DU-optimization and adaptive-pathways lineage that this study plugs into — search across a deeply uncertain ensemble, re-evaluate on an independent ensemble, then discover/adapt. These define the applied workflow against which the ensemble-design intervention is positioned, and they all inherit the i.i.d./probabilistic search-ensemble construction the proposed hazard-space design replaces. The framework underpinning them (MORDM) and the robustness metrics they use are in [objective & robustness formulations](objective_and_robustness_formulations.md).

---

- **Trindade, B. C., Reed, P. M., Herman, J. D., Zeff, H. B. & Characklis, G. W. (2017).** Reducing regional drought vulnerabilities and multi-city robustness conflicts using many-objective optimization under deep uncertainty. *Advances in Water Resources*, 104, 195-209. doi:10.1016/j.advwatres.2017.03.023
  Foundational paper establishing DU-in-search within MORDM: instead of optimizing on well-characterized-uncertainty (WCU) streamflow ensembles and only stress-testing afterward, the Borg MOEA searches directly across an ensemble of deeply uncertain states of the world (SOWs), sampled by Latin hypercube over 13 deep-uncertainty factors (demand growth, evaporation, capacity, costs) combined with Kirsch-style synthetic streamflows (Cholesky + bootstrap resampling). Reservoir policies are adaptive risk-of-failure (ROF) action triggers: each week, year-long (52-week) ROF simulations estimate the probability combined storage falls below critical levels, firing restrictions, transfers, and financial instruments. Five regional objectives (reliability, restriction frequency, Jordan Lake allocation, average and worst-case cost). Robustness via satisficing over the SOW ensemble; PRIM/CART scenario discovery maps vulnerabilities. Four-utility Research Triangle case. Key finding: stressing triggers with the comprehensive DU ensemble during search fundamentally changes perceived tradeoffs and substantially improves individual and regional robustness; regional demand-growth rate dominates robustness, and demand-growth control yields up to ~30% robustness gains.

- **Trindade, B. C., Reed, P. M. & Characklis, G. W. (2019).** Deeply uncertain pathways: Integrated multi-city regional water supply infrastructure investment and portfolio management. *Advances in Water Resources*, 134, 103442. doi:10.1016/j.advwatres.2019.103442
  Introduces the DU Pathways framework, the mature end-to-end workflow this study plugs into: it couples ROF decision rules (52-week rolling ROF simulations on the prior year's demands) with dynamic adaptive policy pathways and time-evolving information feedbacks to yield management-conditioned infrastructure pathways. Workflow stages: cooperative DU optimization with Borg over LHS-sampled deep uncertainties (the DU sampling scheme pairs synthetic streamflow/evaporation/demand realizations with deep-uncertainty draws, contrasted with WCU sampling that appends only best-projected DU values), then DU re-evaluation of Pareto solutions on a broader independent ensemble (e.g., 2000 SOWs across 1000 hydrologic realizations), then enhanced scenario discovery using boosted trees. Argues this rule-based form scales to dozens of uncertainties, avoiding the curse of dimensionality of tree-structured real-options analysis. Four-utility Research Triangle (Raleigh, Durham, Cary, Chapel Hill). Identifies cost-effective near-term infrastructure options, the uncertainties driving tradeoffs and robustness conflicts, and interdependent multi-actor failure modes.

- **Gold, D. F., Reed, P. M., Gorelick, D. E. & Characklis, G. W. (2022).** Power and pathways: Exploring robustness, cooperative stability, and power relationships in regional infrastructure investment and water supply management portfolio pathways. *Earth's Future*, 10(2), e2021EF002472. doi:10.1029/2021EF002472
  Extends DU Pathways with a Regional Defection Analysis (RDA) to assess cooperative stability and map power relationships among partners. The standard DU workflow (cooperative DU optimization with Borg over LHS deep uncertainties plus Kirsch synthetic streamflows; storage-ROF triggers defined as the probability storage drops below 20% capacity over the next 52 weeks; DU re-evaluation on a larger independent SOW ensemble; satisficing robustness approximating Starr's domain criterion over objectives such as reliability, restriction frequency, net present cost, peak and worst-first-percentile financial cost) is augmented by a second, individual many-objective Borg search per utility that explores how each partner could defect from the regional compromise. This maps each actor's power and agency to degrade cooperating partners' performance, showing that power imbalances and defection incentives can destabilize otherwise robust regional alternatives. Demonstrated on the three-utility hypothetical Sedento Valley benchmark; reveals complex regional power relationships and suggests ways to improve cooperative stability.

- **Gold, D. F., Reed, P. M., Gorelick, D. E. & Characklis, G. W. (2023).** Advancing regional water supply management and infrastructure investment pathways that are equitable, robust, adaptive, and cooperatively stable. *Water Resources Research*, 59(9), e2022WR033671. doi:10.1029/2022WR033671
  Current state of the art in the DU Pathways lineage: DU Pathways-ERAS adds equity, adaptive capacity, and time-evolving vulnerability measures to robustness and cooperative stability. Uses the same workflow backbone — Multi-master (MM) Borg cooperative DU optimization over an approximate LHS deep-uncertainty sampling paired with Kirsch synthetic streamflows, ROF triggers (restriction, transfer, infrastructure) on 52-week storage-failure probabilities, then DU re-evaluation of pathway policies on a broader independent full-DU SOW ensemble with percent-satisficing robustness — and adds new tools for adaptive capacity and scenario discovery of time-evolving failure modes. Six-utility Research Triangle case. Key findings: commonly employed robustness framings (e.g., maximizing regional aggregate) can have large unintended adverse consequences for individual partners and regional equity; regional and individual vulnerabilities are highly interdependent, motivating cooperative agreements that limit counterparty risk. Findings are positioned as broadly applicable to cooperative infrastructure planning globally.
