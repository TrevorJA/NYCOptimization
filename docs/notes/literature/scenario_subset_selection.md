# Scenario subset selection from large ensembles

*Paper 3 literature note. Annotated summaries of items in the Zotero collection "Paper 3 NYC Reoptimization" (`ISYGLK35`). Last updated 2026-06-16.*

Scope: methods for choosing a small, informative, or representative **subset** from a larger candidate ensemble, and for diagnosing how subset size and coverage affect downstream conclusions. This is the methodological home of the proposed space-filling hazard-space subsample; Bonham et al. (2024) is the closest published precedent. Probability-representative reduction from the stochastic-programming tradition is in [scenario reduction (stochastic programming)](scenario_reduction_stochastic_programming.md); the effect of finite ensembles on optimizer reliability is in [sampling noise & overfitting](sampling_noise_and_overfitting.md).

---

- **Bonham, N., Kasprzyk, J., Zagona, E. & Rajagopalan, B. (2024).** Subsampling and space-filling metrics to test ensemble size for robustness analysis with a demonstration in the Colorado River Basin. *Environmental Modelling & Software*, 172, 105933. doi:10.1016/j.envsoft.2023.105933
  Simulates once over a master states-of-the-world ensemble (Colorado River shortage policies; 500-scenario ensemble from a streamflow x demand x storage factorial reduced by conditioned Latin Hypercube Sampling, cLHS), then statistically subsamples the results database into smaller a priori (policy-free) ensembles of varying size, recomputing robustness and re-ranking with no extra model runs. Coverage is scored by three model-free space-filling metrics: minimum pairwise Euclidean distance (mindist) and the mean and SD of minimum-spanning-tree (MST) edge lengths, scaled relative to the full set. Rank stability vs the full ensemble is measured with Kendall's tau-b. Accurate rankings emerge with as few as 50-300 scenarios (50 vulnerability, 250 reliability, 300 resiliency satisficing); replicate ensembles per size are recommended. The closest precedent for this study; key difference is that it subsamples the *post-hoc robustness-analysis* ensemble, whereas RQ1 moves space-filling subsampling into the *search* ensemble.

- **Giudici, F., Castelletti, A., Giuliani, M. & Maier, H. R. (2020).** An active learning approach for identifying the smallest subset of informative scenarios for robust planning under deep uncertainty. *Environmental Modelling & Software*, 127, 104681. doi:10.1016/j.envsoft.2020.104681
  Introduces ROSS (Robust Optimal Scenario Selection), an active-learning loop that adaptively grows the smallest informative scenario subset inside robust optimization. It iteratively samples candidate subsets from an evolving multivariate Gaussian distribution over scenario-space coordinates, scores each by the optimization-derived robustness (R) metric (minimax / satisficing), and reweights the distribution toward high-scoring (most informative) regions until convergence (90% of draws reach the maximum score). Selection is therefore outcome-/robustness-informed, not policy-free, and requires simulation during search. Demonstrated on robust planning of an off-grid hybrid (diesel + renewables + storage) energy system, cutting computation 23-84% vs full-ensemble robust optimization while isolating very small informative regions. The closest existing competitor: it selects scenarios adaptively by informativeness, whereas the proposed design fixes an a priori space-filling sample.

- **Carlsen, H., Lempert, R., Wikman-Svahn, P. & Schweizer, V. (2016).** Choosing small sets of policy-relevant scenarios by combining vulnerability and diversity approaches. *Environmental Modelling & Software*, 84, 155-164. doi:10.1016/j.envsoft.2016.06.011
  Combines, for the first time, vulnerability-based selection (scenario-discovery clusters of futures where policies fail; outcome-informed) with diversity-based selection (numerical optimization choosing the set that maximizes the summed pairwise distance over discrete categorical scenario variables; policy-free maximin-style coverage). Criteria are introduced stepwise to expose their separate influence. Applied to climate-resilient infrastructure across three African river basins (Volta, Orange, Zambezi), reducing an ensemble of 121 futures to six representative climate futures (river-basin simulation cost capped selection at <=6 per basin). The canonical "which scenarios" reference; the proposed LHS-in-hazard-space design is its continuous, ensemble-scale, fully a priori analogue.

- **McPhail, C., Maier, H. R., Westra, S., Kwakkel, J. H. & van der Linden, L. (2020).** Impact of scenario selection on robustness. *Water Resources Research*, 56(9), e2019WR026515. doi:10.1029/2019WR026515
  First systematic, quantitative methodology for how the choice of evaluation scenarios affects both robustness values and the ranking of alternatives, illustrated on the Lake Problem. Scenario-set composition has a large effect on absolute robustness values but a comparatively small effect on rankings of alternatives (ranking similarity measured via Kendall's tau correlation, the metric Bonham later reuses), traced to complex interactions among scenario-space coverage, the system performance metric, and the robustness metric. Strong evidence that scenario-set composition matters, with the caution that composition effects must be shown on decision-relevant outputs (rankings), not robustness values alone.

- **Lutz, A. F., ter Maat, H. W., Biemans, H., Shrestha, A. B., Wester, P. & Immerzeel, W. W. (2016).** Selecting representative climate models for climate change impact studies: an advanced envelope-based selection approach. *International Journal of Climatology*, 36(12), 3988-4005. doi:10.1002/joc.4608
  Three-step sequential envelope-plus-skill selection from a ~70-94 GCM-run CMIP5 pool per RCP. Step 1 (envelope on means): for the four corners (cold/warm x dry/wet) defined by the 10th/90th percentiles of projected delta-T and delta-P, the five nearest model runs per corner are selected by Euclidean distance in percentile space (5 x 4 = 20). Step 2 (envelope on extremes): ranked ETCCDI extreme indices (R99pTOT, CDD, WSDI, CSDI) reduce each corner to two (eight runs). Step 3 (past-performance skill): PDF-overlap and precipitation skill scores plus a monsoon-bias score reduce each corner to one, yielding four representative models per RCP. Policy-free, a priori coordinate selection in an impact-relevant metric space that deliberately includes extremes; precedent for choosing subset members by location in a hazard-like space, though by deterministic corner-nearest rather than uniform space-filling.

- **Lamontagne, J. R., Reed, P. M., Link, R., Calvin, K. V., Clarke, L. E. & Edmonds, J. A. (2018).** Large ensemble analytic framework for consequence-driven discovery of climate change scenarios. *Earth's Future*, 6(3), 488-504. doi:10.1002/2017EF000701
  Generates a full-factorial master ensemble of 33,750 GCAM scenarios (3,750 SSP "worlds" over six aggregated socioeconomic factors x nine CO2-price/SPA regimes), then discovers consequential subsets a posteriori ("explore/generate first, choose later") via parallel-axis visual factor mapping plus statistical classification (scenario-discovery / sensitivity analysis) of which factor combinations drive user-specified outcomes (e.g., high/low mitigation cost). Selection is outcome-driven and requires the full simulation database first. Conventional reference-scenario selection (five canonical SSPs) misses policy-relevant hybrids of optimistic/pessimistic assumptions. Supports the very-large-master-ensemble-then-principled-reduction architecture and the claim that small default sets under-sample consequential regions.
