# Sampling noise, ensemble size & overfitting in policy search

*Paper 3 literature note. Annotated summaries of items in the Zotero collection "Paper 3 NYC Reoptimization" (`ISYGLK35`). Last updated 2026-06-16.*

Scope: how a **finite evaluation ensemble** affects optimizer reliability and out-of-sample generalization — the convergence-reliability and robustness hypotheses. A fixed finite scenario set makes fitness deterministic but biased; the structure of that set governs both the bias and how well solutions generalize. Supports the ensemble size / replicate / seed decision. Subset-selection methods that reduce ensemble size are in [scenario subset selection](scenario_subset_selection.md); the MOEA reliability machinery is in [MOEA methods](moea_methods.md).

---

- **Brodeur, Z. P., Herman, J. D. & Steinschneider, S. (2020).** Bootstrap aggregation and cross-validation methods to reduce overfitting in reservoir control policy search. *Water Resources Research*, 56(8), e2020WR027184. doi:10.1029/2020WR027184
  Imports bootstrap aggregation (bagging) and calibration-validation-testing from machine learning to curb policy-search overfitting to the specific flood/drought sequence in training hydrology. Folsom Reservoir; binary policy trees (CART-like) optimized by genetic programming. A K-nearest-neighbor block bootstrap of a 1,113-yr tree-ring paleo-inflow record builds a 30-member ensemble of 35-yr daily sequences (training 1982-2016; held-out test 1923-1981). A 2×2×2 incomplete factorial fixes compute at 30 policies × 10,000 NFE each and varies: calibrate to one historical sequence vs. one bootstrapped sequence per policy; aggregate by best-tree vs. ensemble-mode voting; score on calibration vs. validation. Key finding: selecting the best policy by validation (not calibration) performance most improves out-of-sample water-supply/flood objectives; ensemble-mode voting needs policy diversity (from bootstrapping) to compete. Precedent for resampled finite-length training sets generalizing better than one fixed record — the mechanism behind the robustness hypothesis, and the **primary anchor for the `resampled_probabilistic` design** (the Trindade/Gold lineage is cited only for the principle of re-randomizing the search ensemble; see `scenario_design_tables.md`, Table 3).
  **Important limit on what this paper can be cited for: it defines no overfitting-gap metric.** Overfitting is diagnosed **graphically** — objective distributions over the training and the held-out test ensembles plotted side by side — with no gap equation, no gap magnitude, and no gap-based ranking anywhere in the paper. Brodeur further restricts all claims to *relative* rankings within each period and never interprets the absolute train-vs-test difference, precisely because the two ensembles are not draws from the same distribution. A search-minus-test "overfitting gap" is therefore not a citable comparison metric, and is excluded here (`objective_definitions.md` §3.3).

- **Taylor, W., Brodeur, Z. P., Steinschneider, S. & Herman, J. D. (2026).** Comparing the robustness of forecast-informed reservoir operating policies under forecast uncertainty and hydrologic extremes. *Journal of Water Resources Planning and Management*, 152(4), 04026005. doi:10.1061/JWRMD5.WRENG-7250
  Extends the Brodeur logic from streamflow realizations to *forecast* realizations at Oroville (CA): forecast-informed (EFO) risk curves are trained either on the single HEFS hindcast record or on 100 synthetic forecast ensembles (one independently trained policy per ensemble, from the Brodeur & Steinschneider generator), then tested on the four largest floods rescaled to 100/200/300-yr magnitudes under held-out forecast ensembles. Synthetic-trained policies require the smallest flood pool under both HEFS and synthetic forecasts and are least sensitive to forecast uncertainty — "training on synthetic forecasts can help avoid overfitting to a single forecast ensemble" — while training on the single observed record yields a policy that hedges only a few days ahead of the peak. Notable secondary result: withholding the largest recorded flood from training barely changes the policy, and they argue the informative events to withhold are those with the *least accurate forecasts*, not the largest magnitude. The strongest recent support for the resampled-probabilistic design; its ensemble treatment is replication without coverage (no in-search aggregation, no designed set), so it establishes the floor that designed hazard coverage must beat (full note `notes/Taylor et al. (2026).md`).

- **Zatarain Salazar, J., Reed, P. M., Quinn, J. D., Giuliani, M. & Castelletti, A. (2017).** Balancing exploration, uncertainty and computational demands in many objective reservoir optimization. *Advances in Water Resources*, 109, 196-210. doi:10.1016/j.advwatres.2017.09.014
  Diagnostic assessment of parallel (master-worker vs. multi-master) Borg MOEA configurations for EMODPS with RBF policies on the Lower Susquehanna, optimizing over differently sized Monte Carlo ensembles of synthetic streamflow/evaporation (Kirsch monthly generation, Nowak daily disaggregation). Three realizations-per-evaluation levels (50/500/1000) are drawn from 10,000 stochastic traces by 1-D Latin hypercube sampling of the inverse empirical CDF of annual Marietta flow, with the wettest/driest years forced in; objectives aggregated by minimax (worst-case realization). Borg uses ε-dominance archiving, auto-adaptive operators/restarts; 25 random-seed trials; reliability judged by hypervolume and attainment (probability of meeting hypervolume thresholds) across core counts 32-256. Larger ensembles sharpen statistical fidelity but raise cost and noise; multi-master cooperative search sustains reliability and reduces the need for many seeds. Nearest published precedent for realized-metric coordinates during search, but only 1-D and probability-preserving, not multi-dimensional uniform-in-hazard coverage.
  **Also the protocol precedent for comparing across search-ensemble levels** (§5.3, Figs. 12-13): because each ensemble size induces a different fitness landscape, the reference set is built **per level**, cross-level MOEA metrics (hypervolume, GD, ε-indicator) are declared **incomparable**, and the levels are compared **only** by re-evaluating their solutions on a common independent verification ensemble; Fig. 13 gates reference-set contribution on that held-out performance. This is the rule adopted for cross-design comparison here — a *pooled* reference set across designs is not a defensible measure (`objective_definitions.md` §4.3).
