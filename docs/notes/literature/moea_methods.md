# Many-objective optimization methods & policy search

*Paper 3 literature note. Annotated summaries of items in the Zotero collection "Paper 3 NYC Reoptimization" (`ISYGLK35`). Last updated 2026-06-16.*

Scope: the optimizer machinery and policy-search framing this study runs on — the Borg / MM-Borg algorithm, MOEA diagnostics, and evolutionary multi-objective direct policy search (EMODPS). These justify algorithm and protocol choices (seeds, NFE, attainment thresholds, parallelization). How a finite evaluation ensemble interacts with these reliability properties is in [sampling noise & overfitting](sampling_noise_and_overfitting.md).

---

- **Reed, P. M., Hadka, D., Herman, J. D., Kasprzyk, J. R. & Kollat, J. B. (2013).** Evolutionary multiobjective optimization in water resources: The past, present, and future. *Advances in Water Resources*, 51, 438-456. doi:10.1016/j.advwatres.2012.01.005
  Field review that also delivers the largest diagnostic benchmarking of MOEAs in water resources at the time (>100,000 runs, trillions of evaluations). Establishes the now-standard diagnostic protocol: Latin hypercube sampling of each algorithm's parameterization space, ~50 random-seed replicates per parameterization, and runtime quality measured by three indicators — generational distance (proximity), additive ε-indicator (consistency), and hypervolume (convergence + diversity, via the attainment function). Introduces control maps (population size vs NFE projections) to convey effectiveness, efficiency, reliability, and controllability, and the notion of "sweet spots" of controllable parameterizations. Benchmarks ten MOEAs across rainfall-runoff calibration, long-term groundwater monitoring, and risk-based water-supply portfolio problems exhibiting many objectives, multimodality, nonlinearity, severe constraints, stochastic objectives, and nonseparability. The standard framing citation for the optimization approach and diagnostic design.

- **Hadka, D. & Reed, P. (2013).** Borg: An auto-adaptive many-objective evolutionary computing framework. *Evolutionary Computation*, 21(2), 231-259. doi:10.1162/EVCO_a_00075
  Introduces the Borg MOEA, unifying an ε-dominance archive, ε-progress (a convergence-speed measure triggering randomized restarts when search stalls), and auto-adaptive multi-operator recombination that adaptively reweights SBX, DE, PCX, SPX, UNDX, and UM operators based on their recent contribution to the archive. Borg is presented as a class of algorithms whose operator mix self-tunes per problem rather than a single fixed algorithm. Benchmarked on 33 instances of 18 DTLZ, WFG, and CEC 2009 test problems using a 1,000-point Latin hypercube of parameterizations evaluated over 50 random-seed trials; Borg meets or exceeds six state-of-the-art MOEAs on the majority. The core optimization algorithm used here. (summary from abstract/metadata; fulltext unavailable)

- **Hadka, D. & Reed, P. (2015).** Large-scale parallelization of the Borg multiobjective evolutionary algorithm to enhance the management of complex environmental systems. *Environmental Modelling & Software*, 69, 353-369. doi:10.1016/j.envsoft.2014.10.014
  Develops two parallel Borg implementations: master-slave (parallel objective evaluation) and multi-master (MM-Borg), an island model in which a dedicated controller node maintains a global ε-dominance archive and seeds/aids master islands, which share solutions and adaptive operator probabilities. Initial LHS populations are partitioned across islands by the controller; runtime data (approximation-set snapshots, operator probabilities, local/global restart frequencies) are logged every 10,000 NFE. Scalability is compared to 16,384 processors on a risk-based water-supply portfolio problem. Master-slave parallel efficiency stalls (hypervolume plateaus near 0.85), whereas MM-Borg scales efficiently to tens of thousands of cores and dramatically improves reliability of attaining high-quality solutions. Justifies the parallel optimizer driving the HPC campaigns.

- **Zatarain Salazar, J., Reed, P. M., Herman, J. D., Giuliani, M. & Castelletti, A. (2016).** A diagnostic assessment of evolutionary algorithms for multi-objective surface water reservoir control. *Advances in Water Resources*, 92, 172-185. doi:10.1016/j.advwatres.2016.04.006
  Diagnostic benchmarking of modern MOEAs supporting EMODPS, in which operating policies are parameterized as global nonlinear approximators (radial basis functions) and optimized by an MOEA. Case study: six-objective Conowingo reservoir, Lower Susquehanna River Basin. Protocol mirrors Reed et al. (2013): LHS over each MOEA's parameter ranges, 30 random-seed replicates, and convergence/consistency/diversity assessed via generational distance, additive ε-indicator, and hypervolume, summarized in control maps of efficiency and controllability. ε-dominance, time-continuation, and auto-adaptive search prove most helpful; the ε-MOEA, auto-adaptive Borg MOEA, and ε-NSGA-II yield superior, reliable results with low parameter sensitivity. Concludes EMODPS is promising but algorithm choice remains critical as complexity grows. Informs algorithm choice for reservoir-policy optimization.

- **Giuliani, M., Castelletti, A., Pianosi, F., Mason, E. & Reed, P. M. (2016).** Curses, tradeoffs, and scalable management: Advancing evolutionary multiobjective direct policy search to improve water reservoir operations. *Journal of Water Resources Planning and Management*, 142(2), 04015050. doi:10.1061/(ASCE)WR.1943-5452.0000570
  Canonical EMODPS reference. Frames direct policy search with nonlinear approximating networks optimized by an MOEA as a scalable alternative to stochastic dynamic programming (SDP), circumventing SDP's three curses — dimensionality, modeling, and multiple objectives. Compares two policy architectures, artificial neural networks (ANN) vs radial basis functions (RBF), finding RBF policies more effective for discovering Pareto-approximate policies. Demonstrated on the two-objective (hydropower vs flood risk) multipurpose Hoa Binh reservoir, Vietnam, using the self-adaptive Borg MOEA; EMODPS policies are validated against SDP-based benchmark solutions. The canonical EMODPS reference.

- **Giuliani, M., Herman, J. D., Castelletti, A. & Reed, P. (2014).** Many-objective reservoir policy identification and refinement to reduce policy inertia and myopia in water management. *Water Resources Research*, 50(4), 3355-3377. doi:10.1002/2013WR014700
  Decision-analytic framework combining reservoir policy identification, many-objective optimization under uncertainty, and visual analytics. Policies are parameterized with Gaussian radial basis functions (RBFs) and optimized with the self-adaptive Borg MOEA over six objectives (hydropower revenue, water supply, atomic-plant cooling, environmental flows, etc.) at Conowingo Dam, Lower Susquehanna River. A baseline policy reproducing current releases is identified, then refined via EMODPS. Demonstrates that deterministic historical-inflow policies significantly overestimate system reliability, whereas optimized policies are more robust to hydroclimatic uncertainty while better balancing multisector tradeoffs.

- **Herman, J. D., Quinn, J. D., Steinschneider, S., Giuliani, M. & Fletcher, S. (2020).** Climate adaptation as a control problem: Review and perspectives on dynamic water resources planning under uncertainty. *Water Resources Research*, 56(2), e2019WR025502. doi:10.1029/2019WR025502
  Reviews and classifies dynamic water-planning studies through an optimal-control / reinforcement-learning lens along three axes: uncertainty characterization, policy structure, and solution method — spanning dynamic programming, approximate DP and model predictive control (MPC), parametric rules, and direct policy search / EMODPS. Identifies exogenous (forcing, model-structure, parameter), endogenous (human-environment), and sampling uncertainty due to finite historical and projected records as central gaps, and flags out-of-sample validation, robustness of optimized policies, experimental design, and indicator selection as key opportunities. The high-level framing of the policy-search-under-uncertainty space; its sampling-uncertainty gap directly motivates this study.
