# Stochastic streamflow & weather generation

*Paper 3 literature note. Annotated summaries of items in the Zotero collection "Paper 3 NYC Reoptimization" (`ISYGLK35`). Last updated 2026-06-16.*

Scope: synthetic streamflow and weather generators used to build large hydrologic ensembles, including those that deliberately amplify drought characteristics. These are the candidate engines for the **candidate pools** the hazard-filling designs select from, for the probabilistic and input-stratified designs' generated members, and for the held-out re-evaluation ensemble; they also inform the scenario-length / window-construction decision. Bottom-up exposure-space framing of these generators is in [bottom-up & scenario-neutral design](bottom_up_scenario_neutral.md); the hazard metrics computed on their output are in [hydrologic hazard metrics](hydrologic_hazard_metrics.md).

---

- **Steinschneider, S. & Brown, C. (2013).** A semiparametric multivariate, multisite weather generator with low-frequency variability for use in climate risk assessments. *Water Resources Research*, 49(11), 7205-7220. doi:10.1002/wrcr.20528
  Semiparametric daily multivariate, multisite weather generator with three coupled components: (1) a wavelet decomposition feeding an autoregressive model of annual regional climate to embed structured low-frequency (interannual-to-decadal, Hurst-type) variability; (2) a Markov chain (wet/dry spell statistics) plus k-nearest-neighbor (KNN) bootstrap resampling that preserves cross-variable covariance and spatial correlation across sites; and (3) quantile mapping to impose prescribed long-term distributional shifts. The wavelet-AR signal modulates the Markov/KNN daily process, so parameters in any component can be perturbed to alter lower- and higher-order statistics at daily, seasonal, and interannual scales. Demonstrated over the Connecticut River basin with an ensemble of 50 simulations. Establishes the perturb-generator-parameters-then-stress-test (input-space) pipeline at the weather level.

- **Steinschneider, S., Ray, P., Rahat, S. H. & Kucharski, J. (2019).** A weather-regime-based stochastic weather generator for climate vulnerability assessments of water systems in the western United States. *Water Resources Research*, 55(8), 6923-6945. doi:10.1029/2018WR024446
  Semiparametric weather-regime-conditioned generator with three components: (1) a nonhomogeneous Markov chain simulating daily weather regimes conditioned on global-scale climate features; (2) block bootstrapping plus a Gaussian copula for multivariate, multisite precipitation and temperature; and (3) modules imposing thermodynamic and dynamical climate change, including Clausius-Clapeyron precipitation scaling, elevation-dependent warming, and shifting ENSO dynamics. Developed for cold-season climate in the Tuolumne River Basin, California, where eight weather regimes dominate local climate; validation preserves mean, variance, skew, and extremes, with mild underdispersion in annual totals and a 1-2 day negative bias in spell statistics. A representative modern example of the perturb-generator-parameters (input-space) paradigm the proposed design contrasts against.

- **Herman, J. D., Zeff, H. B., Lamontagne, J. R., Reed, P. M. & Characklis, G. W. (2016).** Synthetic drought scenario generation to support bottom-up water supply vulnerability assessments. *Journal of Water Resources Planning and Management*, 142(11), 04016050. doi:10.1061/(ASCE)WR.1943-5452.0000701
  Adapts the Kirsch et al. (2013) synthetic streamflow generator (Cholesky decomposition of the historical correlation matrix to maintain temporal autocorrelation; bootstrap resampling to preserve multisite spatial correlation) by introducing a weighted bootstrap that preferentially draws the lowest pth quantile of historical years, controllably amplifying drought frequency and severity beyond the record while approximately preserving spatial-temporal correlation. Synthetic droughts are anchored to a stakeholder-salient event (2007-2008 North Carolina drought), demonstrated on an urban water-portfolio design problem and benchmarked against downscaled GCM runoff projections. Canonical bridge between stress testing and MORDM, and direct support for designing hazard diversity rather than relying on the unconditioned record. Also the cleanest evidence that hazard-oriented ensemble design needs **no** deeply uncertain climate input space: the amplification is a reweighting of a stationary generator — the same reasoning that supports running hazard-filling in the stationary population as well as the DU-forced one.

- **Zaniolo, M., Fletcher, S. & Mauter, M. (2024).** FIND: a synthetic weather generator to control drought frequency, intensity, and duration. *Environmental Modelling & Software*, 172, 105927. doi:10.1016/j.envsoft.2023.105927
  Uses a standardized streamflow/drought index (SSI; cumulative flow fit to a Gamma or Pearson-III marginal) to directly and independently control drought frequency, intensity, and duration. Drought segments are swapped/manipulated in index space to hit prescribed F-I-D targets, then the cumulative values are disaggregated back to monthly flows via k-nearest-neighbor (KNN) resampling, with correlated multisite generation preserving cross-site drought concordance and observed hydrologic variability. Open-source code. The most modern statement that drought F-I-D is a natural hazard coordinate system; FIND designs generator targets one profile at a time, whereas the proposed method subsamples realized members by their hazard coordinates.

- **Nazemi, A., Wheater, H. S., Chun, K. P. & Elshorbagy, A. (2013).** A stochastic reconstruction framework for analysis of water resource system vulnerability to climate-induced changes in river flow regime. *Water Resources Research*, 49(1), 291-305. doi:10.1029/2012WR012755
  Stochastic reconstruction framework generating large local-scale streamflow ensembles under controlled flow-regime shifts. Temporal dependence is built with a conditional copula (Sklar's theorem; each flow drawn conditioned on the previous step), marginals enforced by quantile mapping. Regime change is parameterized by just two controls — a shift in the timing of the annual peak and a shift in annual flow volume — so system vulnerability can be visualized as a two-dimensional vulnerability map in that flow-statistic space. Applied to the South Saskatchewan River Basin (Canadian prairies). Bottom-up scenario design in streamflow-statistic (hazard) space rather than climate or generator-parameter space — the same coordinate system proposed here.

- **Brunner, M. I. & Gilleland, E. (2020).** Stochastic simulation of streamflow and spatial extremes: a continuous, wavelet-based approach. *Hydrology and Earth System Sciences*, 24(8), 3967-3982. doi:10.5194/hess-24-3967-2020
  Frequency-domain generator (PRSim.wave) combining an empirical wavelet-transform spatio-temporal model with phase randomization and the flexible four-parameter kappa distribution. Five steps: derive random phases, fit kappa, wavelet transform, inverse wavelet transform, back-transform to kappa. Applying common (shared) phase perturbations across sites reproduces spatial dependence in extremes, while the wavelet basis preserves short- and long-range temporal dependence and non-stationarities and can yield unobserved extreme events. Evaluated on 671 CONUS catchments; distributed as the R package PRSim. A modern alternative generator emphasizing joint multi-site extreme structure — a candidate for the structurally different generator required by the held-out test ensemble.

- **Ji, S. & Ahn, K.-H. (2023).** Temperature change-informed future multisite streamflow generation to support water supply vulnerability assessments under climate change. *Journal of Hydrology*, 624, 129928. doi:10.1016/j.jhydrol.2023.129928
  Nonstationary multisite daily generator that conditions future streamflow on a newly identified nationwide relationship between annual daily-maximum temperature and annual streamflow, avoiding GCM rainfall realization and bias-correction uncertainty. Couples annual and daily simulation through three components: (1) wavelet-decomposition-based autoregressive simulation imposing the regional climate (temperature) covariate; (2) clustering-based spatial-pattern recognition and simulation; and (3) block bootstrapping plus vine-copula simulation for multisite dependence. Applied to multiple basins in South Korea; 1 degC and 2 degC warming scenarios project substantial streamflow decreases, especially at 2 degC. A current example of input-space conditioning that produces hazard diversity only indirectly.
