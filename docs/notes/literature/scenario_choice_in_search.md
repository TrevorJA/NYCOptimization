# Scenario choice during the optimization search

*Paper 3 literature note. Annotated summaries of items in the Zotero collection "Paper 3 NYC Reoptimization" (`ISYGLK35`). Last updated 2026-06-16.*

Scope: papers whose central methodological move is **how scenarios enter the MOEA search phase** — multi-scenario MORDM and the robustness-during-search framing. These are the baselines the proposed hazard-space evaluation ensemble is measured against; they establish that the search-time scenario set is a first-order design choice. Papers about *which* members to select from a larger pool live in [scenario subset selection](scenario_subset_selection.md); the robustness-metric machinery these papers invoke lives in [objective & robustness formulations](objective_and_robustness_formulations.md).

---

- **Watson, A. A. & Kasprzyk, J. R. (2017).** Incorporating deeply uncertain factors into the many objective search process. *Environmental Modelling & Software*, 89, 159-171. doi:10.1016/j.envsoft.2016.12.001
  Extends MORDM by re-running multi-objective search under severe states of the world (SOW) discovered in a prior MORDM application, rather than only a single baseline scenario. Severe SOW are taken as point values drawn from the scenario-discovery (PRIM) boxes that caused a selected solution to fail; uncertainties are both distribution-shape factors (tail-reweighting to emphasize low-inflow/high-demand extremes) and fixed exogenous parameters (initial water rights, demand growth, initial reservoir volume). Each discovered SOW seeds a separate Borg MOEA run; replicate runs vary scenario assumptions so the analyst can interrogate how the chosen severe scenarios reshape decisions. Solution sets are re-evaluated across scenarios with constraints enforced and compared via visual analytics. Case: Lower Rio Grande Valley (Texas) water-supply portfolio. Finding: optimizing under severe conditions yields more adaptable decision-variable values that perform better under extremes than baseline-only search — foundational demonstration that the search-time scenario set is a design choice, not a fixed input.

- **Eker, S. & Kwakkel, J. H. (2018).** Including robustness considerations in the search phase of Many-Objective Robust Decision Making. *Environmental Modelling & Software*, 105, 201-216. doi:10.1016/j.envsoft.2018.03.029
  Inserts a systematic scenario-selection step into multi-scenario MORDM that balances **policy relevance** (scenarios where the reference-scenario optimum fails) and **scenario diversity** (maximizing pairwise distance in the policy-relevant scenario space). Four reference scenarios are selected this way; ε-NSGA-II then runs once per scenario, and merged solutions are re-evaluated over a scenario ensemble using a signal-to-noise robustness metric (mean/standard deviation per outcome). Selected scenarios are benchmarked against randomly selected scenarios. Case: classic shallow-lake pollution problem (four objectives). Finding: distance-based diverse scenario selection increases tradeoff variety and re-evaluated robustness over both single-reference-scenario search and random scenario choice. Its diversity-based discrete selection in scenario space is the conceptual ancestor of a continuous space-filling design in hazard space.

- **Bartholomew, E. & Kwakkel, J. H. (2020).** On considering robustness in the search phase of Robust Decision Making: A comparison of Many-Objective Robust Decision Making, multi-scenario Many-Objective Robust Decision Making, and Many Objective Robust Optimization. *Environmental Modelling & Software*, 127, 104699. doi:10.1016/j.envsoft.2020.104699
  Compares three methods that differ in how much robustness enters search: MORDM (single reference scenario), multi-scenario MORDM (four additional diverse scenarios per Eker & Kwakkel 2018, identified separately per policy formulation), and MORO (robustness optimized directly via the domain criterion over a fixed set of 50 LHS scenarios at every search step). Optimizer is a generational Borg/ε-NSGA-II hybrid; replicate seeds (50/20/10 for MORDM/MS-MORDM/MORO) are merged and re-evaluated over 10,000 LHS scenarios. Tested on three lake-problem policy formulations: intertemporal (static), planned adaptive, and direct policy search. Finding: more in-search robustness yields more robust re-evaluated solutions but lowers reference-scenario performance and raises compute ("price for robustness"); adaptive formulations help robustness without erasing that price; MS-MORDM is a pragmatic middle ground. Supplies the canonical optimize-under-treatments-then-re-evaluate template.

- **Shavazipour, B., Kwakkel, J. H. & Miettinen, K. (2021).** Multi-scenario multi-objective robust optimization under deep uncertainty: A posteriori approach. *Environmental Modelling & Software*, 144, 105134. doi:10.1016/j.envsoft.2021.105134
  Formulates multi-scenario MORO as a single "all-in-one" scenario-based multi-objective problem (k objectives × s scenarios; here 4 objectives × 5 scenarios = 20 outcomes) and solves it a posteriori with a reference-point achievement scalarizing function (Wierzbicki), generating the robust-efficient front by repeatedly solving from varied reference points rather than with an EMO algorithm. Uses the five scenarios of Eker & Kwakkel 2018 (reference plus four diverse) and proposes identifying vulnerable scenarios before any solution generation. Case: classic shallow-lake problem. Finding: the approach guarantees feasibility and robust efficiency across all selected scenarios at lower computational cost and avoids the scenario-dependency and worst-case/aggregation pathologies of standard MORO; the authors note the lake problem poorly exposes robustness-vs-single-scenario tradeoffs, urging better benchmarks. Confirms the field still hand-picks small scenario sets, leaving upstream ensemble construction open.
